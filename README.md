# Product-Level Profitability & Revenue Optimization Analysis
## Tools: R (tidyverse), ggplot2, dplyr
#### Live Report : https://mutkallam.github.io/Product-Profitability-Revenue-Optimization/

In this project, I analyzed product-level revenue, cost, and profitability using a real-world e-commerce dataset to identify key drivers of profit, uncover inefficiencies, and evaluate strategic opportunities for improving overall business performance.

## Key Steps:

Dataset Preparation: Integrated multiple datasets including orders, order items, products, and customers into a unified transactional dataset. Filtered to completed orders and cleaned key fields such as price, freight cost, and product categories. Engineered core financial metrics including revenue, total cost, gross profit, and contribution margin at the order level.

### Exploratory Data Analysis:

Analyzed distributions of revenue, cost, and profit across transactions and product categories.

Identified patterns in customer geography and product performance to understand where profit is generated and where inefficiencies exist.

### Profitability & Category Analysis:

Aggregated performance at the product category level to evaluate total revenue, total profit, and average margins.

Identified top-performing and underperforming categories to highlight areas for strategic focus and potential portfolio optimization.

### Pareto (Profit Concentration) Analysis:

Conducted a Pareto analysis to determine the extent to which profit is concentrated among a small subset of product categories.

Demonstrated that a limited number of categories contribute the majority of total profit, supporting targeted decision-making.

### Margin & Loss Analysis:

Examined the distribution of profit margins across all transactions to identify low-margin and negative-margin orders.

Highlighted operational inefficiencies driven by high freight costs and suboptimal pricing strategies.

### Scenario-Based Optimization:

Simulated a business scenario reducing freight costs by 10% to evaluate the impact on profitability.

Compared baseline profit with adjusted profit to quantify potential gains from operational improvements.

### Key Takeaways:

Profitability is highly concentrated, with a small number of product categories generating the majority of total profit, indicating strong opportunities for focused investment and prioritization.

Freight costs have a significant impact on margins, with many low and negative-margin transactions driven primarily by high shipping expenses.

A 10% reduction in freight costs resulted in an approximate profit increase of $217K, representing a 9.06% lift in total profit, demonstrating a high-impact operational lever.

Several transactions operate at negative margins, suggesting opportunities to improve pricing strategies or remove unprofitable products.

Strategic focus on high-performing categories, combined with cost optimization and margin monitoring, can significantly improve overall business performance.
