Product-Level Profitability & Revenue Optimization Analysis

Olist Brazilian E-Commerce Dataset

Author

Mutkallam Warraich

1 Executive Summary

  • Total baseline profit was $2.40M
  • A 10% reduction in freight costs increased profit by $217K (+9.06%)
  • Profit is highly concentrated in a small number of categories (Pareto effect)
  • Several transactions operate at negative margins, indicating inefficiencies in pricing or cost structure

2 Key Business Insights

  • A small group of product categories drives the majority of profit
  • Freight costs have a material impact on profitability
  • Low-margin and negative-margin orders present an opportunity for optimization
  • Pricing and cost control are the primary levers for improving profitability

3 Recommendations

  • Optimize logistics to reduce freight costs (high ROI lever)
  • Identify and adjust pricing for low-margin or loss-making products
  • Focus inventory and marketing efforts on top-performing categories
  • Implement margin monitoring at the SKU level

4.4 Profitability Framework

These will have all the key financial metrics including revenue, cost, profit, and margin being calculated.

master_orders_clean <- master_orders_clean %>%
  mutate(
    revenue = price,
    total_cost = 0.65 * price + freight_value,   # assumed 65% cost
    gross_profit = revenue - total_cost,
    margin_pct = gross_profit / revenue
  )

4.5 Category-Level Performance

This section summarizes revenue and profitability across product categories.

category_kpis <- master_orders_clean %>%
  group_by(product_category_name) %>%
  summarise(
    total_revenue = sum(revenue, na.rm = TRUE),
    total_profit = sum(gross_profit, na.rm = TRUE),
    avg_margin = mean(margin_pct, na.rm = TRUE),
    total_orders = n()
  ) %>%
  arrange(desc(total_profit))

4.10 Scenario Analysis

This section simulates the impact of reducing freight costs on profitability.

scenario <- master_orders_clean %>%
  mutate(
    new_cost = 0.65 * price + (freight_value * 0.9),
    new_profit = price - new_cost
  )

# Compare total profit
baseline_profit <- sum(master_orders_clean$gross_profit)
new_profit <- sum(scenario$new_profit)

profit_lift <- new_profit - baseline_profit
